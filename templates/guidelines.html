Investigator Guidelines

Guidelines for Investigators

  • Ensure confidentiality of all complaints and investigations.
  • Submit investigation reports within the assigned deadlines.
  • Communicate with students only through official channels.
  • Maintain professionalism and impartiality during investigations.
  • Follow the institution's policies and procedures for grievance handling.
  • Document all findings, evidence, and communications related to the case.
  • Respect the rights and dignity of all parties involved.
  • Escalate unresolved or sensitive cases to the Grievance Redressal Committee.
  • Attend training sessions and workshops on grievance redressal and ethics.
  • Provide recommendations for systemic improvements if recurring issues are observed.


Investigation Process

  1. Review the assigned grievance and gather all relevant information.
  2. Interview the complainant, respondent, and any witnesses in a neutral setting.
  3. Collect and verify documentary or digital evidence.
  4. Maintain a detailed log of all actions and communications.
  5. Prepare a clear, unbiased report summarizing findings and recommendations.
  6. Submit the report through the portal for review by the committee or admin.


Best Practices

  • Be approachable and empathetic while maintaining boundaries.
  • Use clear, respectful, and non-judgmental language in all communications.
  • Keep all investigation materials secure and accessible only to authorized personnel.
  • Refrain from discussing cases with unauthorized individuals.
  • Seek guidance from senior committee members if in doubt.


Support & Resources

  • Contact the Grievance Redressal Committee for procedural clarifications.
  • Refer to the FAQs and Privacy Policy for more information.
  • Access counseling or legal support if you experience stress or pressure during investigations.
